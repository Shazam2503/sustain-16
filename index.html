How to Play

  1. Find the four hidden groups — and the one decoy. Every puzzle has 16 words: 15 that belong to groups, and 1 decoy that belongs to none of them. Your job is to find the groups and figure out which word is the fake.
  2. Watch out for the decoy word. It's designed to look like it fits, but it doesn't belong to any group. Select it in a guess and you'll lose a mistake — a warning will pop up to let you know it was in your selection.
  3. Select words you think belong together, then hit Submit. Groups can be 3 or 4 words long — once you've selected enough, the Submit button lights up. Tap a word to select it, tap again to deselect.
  4. Watch the colours reveal themselves. Solve a group correctly and it locks in with a colour showing how tricky it was — green is easiest, then blue, orange, and red is the trickiest. Get it wrong and you lose one of your four mistakes.
  5. Stuck? Use a hint. Tap Hint to reveal one of the hidden group names. You'll still need to figure out which words belong to it — and you can cycle through hints as many times as you like.
  6. Crack all four groups to unmask the decoy. Once every group is solved, the decoy word is finally revealed and added to your panel with its own card. It was hiding in plain sight all along. Come back tomorrow for a new theme and a new decoy to outwit.

Knowledge Nugget 💡

Ecotourism is one of the fastest-growing segments of the global tourism industry, but the term has no single, universally binding legal definition. The International Ecotourism Society defines genuine ecotourism through 7 principles: covering impact reduction, cultural awareness, conservation funding, community empowerment, and low-impact design. Without these, it's just tourism with a green label.
